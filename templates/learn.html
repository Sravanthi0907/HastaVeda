Pataka
(The Flag)
Tap to learn more

PATAKA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Flag". Shows power and triumph.
How to do it:
  1. Hold your hand up at chest level
  2. Extend all fingers straight and close together
  3. Bend your thumb slightly inward

✓ Tip: Keep fingers rigid and together

✗ Avoid: Splaying fingers apart

Mushti
(The Fist)
Tap to learn more

MUSHTI

Single-Hand
What it means: "Fist". Shows strength and power.
How to do it:
  1. Curl all four fingers into your palm tightly
  2. Place your thumb firmly over the middle fingers

✓ Tip: Keep the fist firm but wrist relaxed

✗ Avoid: Sticking thumb straight up

Shikara
(The Peak)
Tap to learn more

SHIKARA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Peak". Represents mountains and authority.
How to do it:
  1. Make a tight fist (Mushti)
  2. Extend only your thumb straight upward

✓ Tip: Thumb must be perfectly rigid and vertical

✗ Avoid: Fingers slipping open from the fist

Chandrakala
(Crescent Moon)
Tap to learn more

CHANDRAKALA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Crescent Moon". Represents the moon and beauty.
How to do it:
  1. Curl middle, ring, and pinky into palm
  2. Point index finger straight up
  3. Extend thumb out to the side (90 degrees)

✓ Tip: Form a perfect 90-degree angle between fingers

✗ Avoid: Thumb drooping or fingers lifting

Mrigasirsha
(Deer Head)
Tap to learn more

MRIGASIRSHA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Deer Head". Represents grace and gentleness.
How to do it:
  1. Start with flat palm (Pataka)
  2. Bend middle three fingers forward at 90 degrees
  3. Keep thumb and pinky pointing straight up like ears

✓ Tip: Make middle fingers flat and horizontal

✗ Avoid: Thumb or pinky curling inward

Alapadma
(Bloomed Lotus)
Tap to learn more

ALAPADMA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Bloomed Lotus". Represents beauty and the sun.
How to do it:
  1. Splay all five fingers wide apart
  2. Curl fingers backward slightly
  3. Create a blooming flower shape with your hand

✓ Tip: Push knuckles forward, pull fingertips back

✗ Avoid: Keeping fingers straight like an open palm

Anjali
(Combined Hands)
Tap to learn more

ANJALI

Double-Hand
What it means: "Salutation" or "Offering". Represents respect, prayer, and greeting.
How to do it:
  1. Bring both palms together at the chest
  2. Keep fingers pointing upward and together
  3. Press palms firmly and evenly against each other

✓ Tip: Keep elbows slightly lifted and wrists close to the chest

✗ Avoid: Letting fingers splay apart or palms separate unevenly

Tripataka
(Three Parts of Flag)
Tap to learn more

TRIPATAKA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Three Parts of a Flag". Represents fire and a crown.
How to do it:
  1. Start with Pataka (flat hand)
  2. Bend only the ring finger down to touch the palm
  3. Keep all other fingers straight and together

✓ Tip: Ring finger must press firmly against the palm

✗ Avoid: Bending other fingers along with the ring finger

Ardhapataka
(Half Flag)
Tap to learn more

ARDHAPATAKA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Half Flag". Represents a knife, two leaves, or a banner.
How to do it:
  1. Start with Tripataka hand
  2. Bend ring and little finger down together
  3. Keep index and middle finger straight and together

✓ Tip: Two extended fingers must stay tightly pressed together

✗ Avoid: Spreading the two raised fingers apart

Kartarimukha
(Scissors / Arrow)
Tap to learn more

KARTARIMUKHA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Scissors Face". Represents separation, opposition, or an arrow.
How to do it:
  1. Start with Ardhapataka
  2. Spread index and middle fingers apart like scissors
  3. Curl ring and pinky into palm, thumb tucked

✓ Tip: Create a wide, even V-shape between the two fingers

✗ Avoid: Letting the curled fingers slip open

Ardhachandra
(Half Moon)
Tap to learn more

ARDHACHANDRA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Half Moon". Represents the moon, prayer, or salutation.
How to do it:
  1. Start with Pataka (flat palm)
  2. Spread thumb wide away from fingers
  3. Keep all four fingers straight and together

✓ Tip: Thumb should stretch as far as possible from index finger

✗ Avoid: Bending or curling any of the four fingers

Shukatunda
(Parrot Beak)
Tap to learn more

SHUKATUNDA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Parrot's Beak". Represents shooting an arrow or a parrot.
How to do it:
  1. Make a fist (Mushti)
  2. Extend the index finger straight forward
  3. Keep thumb pressing over the middle fingers

✓ Tip: Index finger should point directly forward like an arrow

✗ Avoid: Pointing the finger upward instead of forward

Padmakosha
(Lotus Bud)
Tap to learn more

PADMAKOSHA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Lotus Bud". Represents a flower, a round fruit, or a bell.
How to do it:
  1. Spread all five fingers apart
  2. Curve all fingers and thumb inward gently
  3. Create a rounded cupped shape like a blooming bud

✓ Tip: All fingers should curve equally to form a smooth bowl

✗ Avoid: Closing fingers too tight like a fist

Hamsasya
(Swan's Beak)
Tap to learn more

HAMSASYA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Swan's Face". Represents a flower, painting, or stringing beads.
How to do it:
  1. Bring all four fingertips together to meet the thumb tip
  2. Point the joined fingertips forward like a beak
  3. Keep a slight hollow space inside the hand

✓ Tip: All five tips must meet at a single point precisely

✗ Avoid: Letting any finger lag behind the others

Mukula
(Flower Bud)
Tap to learn more

MUKULA

Single-Hand
What it means: "Bud". Represents an unopened flower or offering food to a deity.
How to do it:
  1. Bring all five fingertips together at a single point
  2. Keep the palm slightly open and rounded below
  3. Hold the shape pointing upward like a closed bud

✓ Tip: The palm should feel hollow like cupping water

✗ Avoid: Squeezing fingertips so tight the palm flattens
